2025 Cutoff Analysis

What is Cutoff? The closing rank at which the last seat is allotted in a particular college and branch.

How is it decided? It is generated automatically by the counseling software based on seat availability and student preferences.

Previous Year Trend: Due to increased awareness, top branches like Civil and Electrical in top 10 colleges closed very early.

Expected 2026 Cutoff: Competition is expected to rise. A safe rank for a good government college is under 15,000.

Important Note: Nobody can guarantee the exact cutoff. It dynamically depends on:

  • Number of Students appearing
  • Difficulty Level of the exam
  • Seat Availability
  • Reservation Category constraints
  • Choice Filling patterns of students
  • Counselling Round (1st, 2nd, or Mop-up)

BCECEB Official Board

What is BCECEB? The Bihar Combined Entrance Competitive Examination Board conducts the DCECE (PE/PPE/PM/PMM) and LE exams.

It is the sole authority for Latest Notifications, Results, Online Counselling, Seat Allotment, Downloads, and Official Notices.

PE (Polytechnic Engineering) Counselling

What is PE? A 3-year Diploma course in various engineering branches after 10th grade.

Who can apply? / Eligibility: Students who have passed 10th standard (Matriculation) with a minimum of 35% marks.

Step-by-Step Counselling Process

  1. Registration with Roll Number and Date of Birth
  2. Login using created credentials
  3. Choice Filling (Select colleges and branches)
  4. Choice Locking (OTP verified)
  5. Seat Allotment Result Declaration
  6. Seat Acceptance (Willingness for Upgradation: Yes/No)
  7. Seat Acceptance Fee Payment (Online)
  8. Document Verification at Reporting Center
  9. Reporting to the Allotted College
  10. Final Admission process

LE (Lateral Entry) Counselling

What is LE? Lateral Entry allows direct admission into the 2nd year (3rd semester) of the 3-year Diploma program.

Who can apply? / Eligibility: 12th Science passed students OR 10th + 2 Years ITI passed students.

Direct 2nd Year Admission: Saves one academic year for eligible candidates.

Counselling Steps

  1. Registration on the LE Portal
  2. Choice Filling of preferred colleges
  3. Seat Allotment Publication
  4. Document Verification at nodal center
  5. Reporting to the allotted institution
  6. Final Admission
Open LE Counselling

Important Tips for Candidates

  • Fill Maximum Choices: Do not restrict yourself to 5-10 colleges. Fill as many choices as you can to avoid missing out.
  • Don't Lock Without Checking: Review your choice order multiple times. Top preference should be #1. Once locked, it cannot be edited.
  • Follow Official Notice: Only trust dates and rules published on the official BCECEB website. Do not rely on WhatsApp rumors.
  • Keep Documents Ready: Certificates like Income and Caste take time to generate from the Block office. Apply early.
  • Keep Fee Ready: The seat acceptance fee and college admission fee must be paid on time. Arrange funds beforehand.
  • Check Every Counselling Round: If not allotted in Round 1, wait for Round 2 and Mop-up counseling patiently.
  • Don't Miss Reporting Date: Missing the document verification date at the nodal center will result in seat cancellation.
